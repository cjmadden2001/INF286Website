Tiger ended up choosing to attend Stanford to compete as a student-athlete. In only two years at Stanford, Tiger won a Stanford record tying 11 tournaments, including an incredible 8 wins in 13 starts in 1996. His 11 tournament wins is tied for the most all-time despite only competing in college for two years. Woods was twice named a 1st team All-American - in both 1995 & 1996.

Tiger was the NCAA Division 1 individual champion in 1996 and was named the Jack Nicklaus national player of the year. Woods won roughly 42 percent of the tournaments he competed in while attending Stanford, winning 11 of the 26 he played. He also had a school record round of 61 at the Pac-10 Championships and he finished at par or better in 27 of his 41 college career rounds. His average while at Stanford stands as the third best school average to date.
